Harvard CS171 Final Project

There's No Place Like Home

Exploring the American Housing Landscape through Maslow's Hierarchy of Needs

Background: Throughout history, home has played an important role as a physical, emotional, social, and psychological binding agent in our lives. Housing touches every level of humankind’s fundamental needs. Capturing this concept, Maslow’s hierarchy of needs is a psychological model that maps out tiers of human needs on the basis of psychology, safety, love, esteem, and other factors. Our goal for this project is to explore Maslow’s hierarchy in the lens of housing visualizations. Our visualizations will be exploratory in nature and allow homebuyers to identify insights like housing hot spots, opportunities for bargains, and geographic housing trends.

Maslow's Fourth Tier: Esteem

Homes have also always played important status roles in society -- whether displays of personal wealth, tokens of affections, or monuments to achievement, the homes regularly serve a key role in the way humans seek or bestow esteem...

Status and entertainment

This bubble graph shows the popularity of housing TV shows (represented by circles) as time progresses (plotted on the x-axis), with the size of a given bubble corresponding to the amount of episodes produced. By clicking down on a circle, the thumbnail of the show appears. The timeline can also be scrubbed to restrict the viewing window to a specified time period.

An analysis of the visualization reveals a precipitous increase in the amount of housing TV shows over time, and a corresponding increase in viewer interest in this genre.
